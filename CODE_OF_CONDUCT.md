# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

Agile Tools is intended to be a technically rigorous, respectful collaboration space.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, professional, and constructive.
- Focus critique on ideas, code, and decisions rather than people.
- Assume good intent, ask clarifying questions, and keep disagreements technical.
- Share actionable details when reporting bugs or proposing changes.
- Respect privacy and do not publish secrets, personal data, or private correspondence.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained hostile conduct
- Discrimination, hate speech, or demeaning language about protected groups
- Trolling, personal attacks, bad-faith disruption, or deliberate derailment
- Sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention
- Doxxing, impersonation, or publication of private information without consent
- Spam, repeated off-topic posting, or other conduct that makes participation harder for others

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Repository maintainers may edit, remove, lock, or moderate content that violates this code of conduct. They may also restrict participation when necessary to protect the project and its contributors.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to repository issues, discussions, pull requests, review comments, and other project-managed communication channels.

## Reporting

Report conduct problems to the maintainer through GitHub using the repository or maintainer account contact options. Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as confidentially as practical.

## Enforcement

Violations may result in warnings, comment or content removal, conversation locks, temporary restrictions, or permanent bans depending on severity and pattern of behavior.
